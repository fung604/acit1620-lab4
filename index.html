Made In Italy

Aprilia is an Italian motorcycle manufacturer with a rich history in the world of motorcycling.

Established in 1945 in Noale, Italy, Aprilia initially produced bicycles before venturing into the motorcycle market in the 1960s.

Known for their innovative and high-performance motorcycles, Aprilia has a strong presence in both racing and production bikes.

The company is part of the Piaggio Group, which also owns other renowned brands like Vespa and Moto Guzzi. Aprilia's motorcycles are highly regarded for their sporty designs, cutting-edge technology, and success in various racing competitions.

Competition

Aprilia faces stiff competition in the motorcycle industry, particularly from other renowned Italian motorcycle manufacturers such as Ducati and MV Agusta. Ducati, known for its powerful sport and superbike offerings, is a major rival, with a strong global presence and a rich racing heritage.

On the international stage, Japanese giants like Yamaha, Honda, and Kawasaki are formidable competitors, producing a wide range of motorcycles, including sportbikes, cruisers, and adventure bikes.

European manufacturers like BMW and KTM also provide strong competition, offering innovative technology and a diverse lineup of motorcycles.

Aprilia must continuously innovate and deliver exceptional performance to stay competitive in this highly competitive industry.
